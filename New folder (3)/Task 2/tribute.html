Life & Legacy

Dr. Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, rose from humble beginnings to become one of India's most respected scientists and the 11th President of India (2002-2007).


Known as the 'People's President', Dr. Kalam was loved for his simplicity, wisdom, and dedication to inspiring young minds. He played a crucial role in developing India's first satellite launch vehicle (SLV-III) and was the chief architect of India's integrated guided missile development program.


Even after his presidency, Dr. Kalam continued to inspire millions through his lectures and books. He passed away on July 27, 2015, while doing what he loved most - addressing students at the Indian Institute of Management Shillong.
